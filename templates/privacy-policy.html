Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Last Updated: July 13, 2026

1. Introduction

Lumvi ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our chatbot service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Account Information: Email address, name, password, company name
  • Payment Information: Processed securely through third-party payment providers (we do not store complete credit card numbers)
  • Chatbot Configuration: FAQs, responses, branding settings, widget customizations
  • Communication Data: Messages you send to our support team

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, time spent on platform
  • Device Information: Browser type, operating system, IP address
  • Cookies: Session cookies, preference cookies, analytics cookies
  • Chatbot Interactions: Messages sent to your chatbots, lead data collected

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Payment providers (transaction confirmations)
  • Analytics services (Google Analytics, if enabled)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:

  • Provide and maintain our Service
  • Process your transactions and send receipts
  • Improve and personalize your experience
  • Send service updates and announcements
  • Respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • Monitor usage and analyze trends
  • Detect and prevent fraud or abuse
  • Comply with legal obligations

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 We Share Your Information With:

  • Service Providers: Hosting (Railway), database (Neon), AI processing (Google Gemini, OpenRouter), payment processing (Flutterwave), email services
  • Analytics Partners: To understand how our Service is used
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or to protect our rights

4.2 We Do NOT:

  • Sell your personal information to third parties
  • Share your data for marketing purposes without consent
  • Use your chatbot data to train our AI models
  • Access your conversations without your permission (except for support)

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures:

  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Encrypted password storage (bcrypt hashing)
  • Secure database access controls
  • Regular security updates and patches
  • Limited employee access to personal data
Note: No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data Retention

  • Active Accounts: Data retained while your account is active
  • Deleted Accounts: Data deleted within 30 days of account deletion
  • Backups: May persist in backups for up to 90 days
  • Legal Requirements: Some data may be retained longer to comply with legal obligations

We use cookies for:

  • Functionality Cookies: Remember your preferences

You can control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling essential cookies may affect functionality.

9. White-Label Use and Connected Third-Party Platforms

Lumvi is a white-label platform: our direct customers ("Agencies") deploy chatbots on their own or their clients' websites, where a different group of people — the Agency's own end customers — interact with the chatbot. This section explains how data flows in that relationship.

  • Our role: For data an Agency's end customers provide through an embedded chatbot (messages, names, emails, phone numbers, order or appointment details), Lumvi acts as a data processor on the Agency's behalf. The Agency is the data controller for that data and is responsible for their own end-user-facing privacy disclosures — this Privacy Policy does not substitute for that.
  • Connected platforms: An Agency may connect their own Shopify, WooCommerce, Acuity, Calendly, or Square account, or configure custom integrations to their own systems ("Agent Actions"). When they do, the chatbot may read from or write to those systems — for example, looking up an order's status or submitting a cancellation request — using credentials the Agency provides and controls. We do not control what an Agency connects or what data those systems hold; the Agency is responsible for having the right to share that data with us for this purpose.
  • What we do with it: Data exchanged with an Agency's connected platforms is used solely to operate that Agency's chatbot and is not combined across Agencies or used to train models.
If you are an Agency: you are responsible for disclosing to your own end customers what data your chatbot collects and how it's used, including any platforms you've connected.
